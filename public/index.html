Kyiv Investment Forum 2025:
Berlin Platform

  • Building upon the momentum of previous editions, the 9th Kyiv Investment Forum will take place in Berlin on November 12, 2025 — uniting bold ideas, global leaders, and breakthrough partnerships in one of Europe’s most dynamic cities.
  • This year’s Forum is more than a conversation — it’s a shared commitment. Hosted in Berlin, a symbol of resilience and innovation, the event will spotlight how Kyiv’s recovery and Europe’s future are deeply connected.
  • Under the theme “Investing in Resilience. Shaping the Future,” we’ll activate new opportunities for investment, collaboration, and long-term impact.

KIF 2025:
Berlin Platform

Uniting global investors, private sector leaders, visionary changemakers, and resilient cities to drive Ukraine’s recovery, scale social impact, and foster inclusive growth — strengthening city-to-city cooperation as a foundation for a more united and resilient Europe.

Our goals:

Accelerate the growth of Kyiv’s investment ecosystem by uniting global investors to support infrastructure recovery and the modernization of Greater Kyiv.
Promote peer-to-peer exchange among European metropolises to share effective strategies for urban investment and resilience.
Highlight the power of social impact and innovation to drive inclusive growth, support Ukraine’s recovery, and contribute to a sustainable, equitable economy aligned with EU integration.
Foster collaboration between the public and private sectors to develop and implement solutions for the economic and social recovery of the Kyiv metropolitan area.

1 Brussels Platform: Sustainable Revival of Greater Kyiv
Attendance:

Reached over 500 attendees on-site, including more than 27 ambassadors and diplomatic representatives, 10 city delegations, EU policymakers, business leaders, and international financial institutions.

More than 2,000 participants joined via livestream.

Place:

City of Brussels, Belgium

Data:

16 November 2023

  • Opened by Ursula von der Leyen with a strong message of support for Kyiv’s transformation.
  • New Brussels–Kyiv Cooperation Memorandum signed to boost reconstruction and partnership.
  • Extended the Brussels Declaration: Barcelona, Tirana, and Metropolia Grand Paris joined the city platform for solidarity and joint urban development
  • European Investment Bank and Kyiv City Council signed agreement to modernize Kyiv’s metro system, supported by a €480,000 technical assistance grant.
  • Kyiv–Brussels business chambers signed cooperation agreement to foster investment.
  • Clear shift from aid to investment – positioning Kyiv as a partner in Europe’s shared future.
